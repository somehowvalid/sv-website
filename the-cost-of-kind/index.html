questions

common questions.

Is there a journal specifically for people pleasers? +

The Cost of Kind was made specifically for people pleasers — people who have spent a long time being kind to everyone except themselves. It does not give advice or prescribe change. It creates space to see the pattern clearly: where it came from, what it has cost, and what reclaiming yourself might feel like. Thirty days. No pressure to arrive anywhere.

What if I have never journaled before? +

The Cost of Kind is not a journaling programme. Each day has one compass entry to read and one question to sit with. Writing is optional — the space is there if you want it, and not required if you don't. There is nothing to complete correctly. It works at whatever pace is yours.

What makes this different from a regular self-help book? +

somehow valid. is not a self-help brand. The collections do not advise, instruct, or tell you what to do next. They are built to help you see what you have been carrying — clearly, and without judgment. There are no tips, no action plans, no before-and-after. The writing treats you as someone who already knows what is true. It simply creates space for you to look at it.

Can I do this if I am going through burnout or exhaustion? +

Yes — this collection was made for exactly that. The first ten days (The Weight) ask nothing of you except to look. No reframing, no fixing. Just the honest weight of it, named and given room. You do not need energy to begin. You need only the willingness to see.

Everything is digital, delivered instantly after purchase. The Compass and Anchor Deck are PDF files — read on any device or print at home. The Frames are a ZIP of 30 PNG images for your lock screen, desktop, or wall. No app required. $27 total for all three parts.
